# Shot Language Reference

Use this file when creating shot lists, storyboards, camera direction, or editing rhythm.

## Shot Sizes

- Extreme wide shot: establish world scale, geography, loneliness, epic scope.
- Wide shot: show character in environment and action path.
- Full shot: show full body action, blocking, posture, costume.
- Medium shot: show interaction, gesture, and readable performance.
- Close-up: show emotion, detail, decision, object importance.
- Extreme close-up: isolate a symbol, eye, hand, button, tear, texture.

## Camera Angles

- Eye level: neutral, intimate, human.
- Low angle: power, wonder, intimidation, scale.
- High angle: vulnerability, overview, isolation.
- Over-the-shoulder: relationship, dialogue, discovery.
- Top-down: pattern, map logic, ritual, choreography.
- Dutch angle: unease, instability, noir or thriller tension.

## Camera Movement

- Static: clarity, stillness, tension, tableau.
- Slow push-in: realization, intimacy, emotional focus.
- Pull-back: reveal loneliness, consequence, larger world.
- Pan: discover space, follow attention, connect elements.
- Tilt: reveal height, scale, transformation.
- Tracking/dolly: follow journey, momentum, action continuity.
- Handheld feel: urgency, realism, instability.
- Orbit: showcase character/object; use sparingly because AI video may distort.

## Editing Rhythm

- 30 seconds: 4-7 shots; one clear idea, minimal subplot.
- 60 seconds: 6-10 shots; clean setup, turn, payoff.
- 90 seconds: 10-14 shots; room for emotional reversal.
- 180 seconds: 14-24 shots; use sequences and recurring motifs.

## Feasibility Rules for AI Video

- Prefer one primary subject action plus one camera movement per shot.
- Split a shot when it requires multiple character interactions, complex choreography, or precise object handoffs.
- Use static or slow camera when facial consistency matters.
- Use wider shots for full-body action; use closeups for emotion and object detail.
- Avoid long continuous shots with changing locations, multiple scale shifts, or fast cuts inside one prompt.

## Shot Table Fields

Use these fields by default:

`镜头 | 时长 | 镜头目的 | 画面描述 | 景别 | 机位 | 摄影机运动 | 主体动作 | 情绪 | 光影 | 转场 | 声音 | 难度 | 修正建议`

## Difficulty Labels

- Low: one subject, stable camera, simple environment motion.
- Medium: subject plus camera movement, moderate environmental detail, simple interaction.
- High: multiple subjects, fast motion, precise choreography, transformation, crowds, water/fire/smoke, or continuity-critical faces.

For high-difficulty shots, include a fallback: simplify motion, use a closeup, split into two shots, or turn a complex action into an implied before/after edit.
